Covid-19 Research Efforts

Covid-19 Research Efforts by IISc and IITs

Following are some of the efforts being put by IITs and IISc to solve the issues of urgent need with respect to Covid-19.
This is a personal collection of the information from different sources which are unverified.
This collection could be of help for collaborative research.

IISc: Source
  • 3D printed valves for split use of ventilators to serve multiple patients
    Lead: Akshay Naik
  • Cyclone separator design for compressor exit flow oil and dust particle cleanup
    Lead: Santosh Hemachandra
  • Drones for disinfection
    Lead: Abhishek Barman
  • GoCoronaGo: contact tracing app and network analytics
    Lead: Tarun Rambha and Yogesh Simmhan
  • Increasing oxygen concentration for ventilators
    Lead: Praveen C Ramamurthy
  • Mobile diagnostic testing lab for COVID-19
    Lead: Sai Siva Gorthi
  • Modeling of epidemic spread in Indian urban conditions
    Lead: Rajesh Sundaresan
  • N95 mask renewal
    Lead: Srinivasan Raghavan;
  • Project Praana: Open Source Ventilator Development
    Lead: Gaurab Banerjee
  • Sub INR 50,000 Ventilator
    Lead: Srinivasan Raghavan
  • UV-based disinfecting device
    Lead: Praveen C Ramamurthy
IIT Tirupati:
  • Cost effective 3D Printable N95 mask
  • Statistical analysis and prediction of spread of infection
  • Low-cost ventilator
  • Spreader tracking system
  • Bioinformatics research for drug discovery
IIT Guwahati:
  • Waterproof protective gear that comes with an antiviral coating.
  • 3D-printed prototype of a full-face shield with scaled up production features.
  • Robotics for patient screening and medicin delivery (a long term plan)
  • Drones for spraying disinfection and surveillance (a long term but can be used for Covid-19)
  • DNA analysis equipment for Covid-19.
  • They have taken up works on several other Covid-19 medical need.
IIT Delhi:
  • Capacity building to produce order of 1 Lakhs N95 masks a day.
  • Developing a low cost testing kit (the kit is under testing).
  • Anti-viral decontamination of inanimate surfaces
    (DST supported project; Team: B S Butola)
IIT Hyderabad:
  • Proposed to use bag valve mask as an added alternative to ventilator.
  • Simulator to predict the transmission of Covid-19
    Lead: Vikram Pudi Simulator
IIT Kanpur:
  • Isolation of the RNA of Corona virus in search for vaccines (in collaboration with IIT Indore)
  • Novel material, cost effective, with anti-viral coating
    (DST supported project, Team: Nagma Praveen) (JNCSR is also carrying out a similar work)
  • Low-cost ventilator
    (Team: Amitabh Bandyopadhyay)
IIT Roorkee:
  • Herbal based hand sanitiser being developed and prepared.
  • Low-cost ventilator prana-vayu
IIT Bombay:
  • Corontine: an application to track the infected patient (its a mobile based app)
    (Similar work also being done by IIT Ropar)
  • Indentifying unique biomarkers of patient infected by Covid-19
    (a DST supported project, Team: Sanjeev Srivastava)
  • Developing anti-bodies against SARS-Cov-2
    (DST supported project; Team: Kiran Kondabagli)
IIT Ropar:
  • An application to track the patient, spreader, carrier etc.
IIT Madras:
  • Face shield manufacturing
  • Medical equipments (Team: Satya Chakravarthy)
